Accessibility & Safeguards

Principles

  • Predictable routines: visual schedule, first-then board, clear transitions.
  • Sensory-aware environment: low-stim corner, dimmable lamp, noise-reduction options.
  • Multiple communication modes: AAC boards, yes/no cards, gestures, short verbal.
  • Education-only scope: we prepare and rehearse; licensed providers do anything clinical.

Clinic-Prep specifics

  • “Meet the tools” at low volume: mirror, air/water, suction sound (20–40 dB).
  • Choice-based rehearsal with timers and a visible break card.
  • Caregiver micro-lesson: how to request accommodations and plan a quiet hour.

Ortho Lab specifics

  • No intraoral anything. All demos on rigs/models (brackets, wires, elastics, spring scales).
  • Gloves and eye protection for certain builds; no aerosols, no powders.
  • Students predict → measure → adjust; rigs are cleaned and stored after each session.

Safety & privacy

  • Two-adult rule; sign-in/out; incident log; consent forms for minors and media.
  • No public diagnosis labels; use first names only; faces blurred in photos.
  • Mandatory reporter obligations followed per site policy.
